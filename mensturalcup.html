Menstrual Cup

Menstrual cups are reusable and easily accessible through supermarkets or other stores that carry menstruation products. They collect your flow, instead of absorbing it like tampons or pads. To decide which size to use, you and your doctor should consider your age, the length of your cervix, whether or not you have a heavy flow, and if you’ve given birth vaginally.

How to use a Menstrual Cup

To insert a menstrual cup:

  1. Wash your hands thoroughly with a fragrance free soap
  2. Apply water or a water-based lube to the rim of the cup for easier application
  3. Holding it in one hand with the rim facing up, tightly fold the menstrual cup in half
  4. Insert the cup, rim up, into your vagina a few inches below your cervix
  5. Once the cup is in your vagina, rotate it to create an airtight seal that stops leaks

To remove a menstrual cup:

  1. Wash your hands thoroughly with a fragrance-free soap
  2. Place your index finger and thumb into your vagina. Pull the stem of the cup gently until you can reach the base.
  3. Pinch the base to release the seal and pull down to remove the cup
  4. Once it’s out, empty the cup into the sink or toilet.
